Islam, media subject
Methodology

1. Introduction

1.1 Definitions

Corpus : set of texts used as a basis for textual analysis.
Occurrence : number of time the term appears in a document or set of documents.
Lemma: chain of signs forming a semantic unit.

1.2. Goals

This methodological note aims for publishing all the treatments used to obtain the data presented in this study. These methods involve certain biases, which are, as far as possible, explained in the paragraphs relating to the parties they affect.

2. Constitution of the corpus

The corpus analyzed here includes all press articles containing the terms "Islam" or "Muslim" between 01.01.1997 and 31.12.2015 and published by Libération, Le Monde and Le Figaro.
We mean by corpus all the press articles of the three dailies and published over the whole period.
By sub-corpus we mean press articles published for the whole period but attached to one of the three dailies only.

The analyzed corpus does not contain the title, the publication heading or the quality of the content (report, analysis, editorial…).

Those temporal bounds make possible to have the necessary distance to measure the impact of 9/11 attacks which marked a turning point in the treatment of Islam in the media.

The choice of those media corresponds to the wish to measure possible differences in treatment within the three "big" French daily newspapers: Le Monde, considered as "the reference", Le Figaro marked on the right of the political chessboard and Liberation rather located on the left.
This corpus is not exhaustive and does not take into account the entire ideological spectrum of the generalist daily press (L'Humanité, La Croix, L'Opinion, Les Echos, Aujourd'hui en France), the conclusions are not intended to be extrapolated, they nevertheless constitute a good first approach to this question. It nonetheless provides a broad view of the orientations of the press regarding the treatment of Islam.

3. Cleaning the corpus

The corpus contains duplicates. To minimize artificial biases due to minor content changes, we have removed duplicate articles from the study corpus.

Considering two articles having their contents similar to (at least) 70% and published less than 3 days apart as duplicates, we only kept the one with the most recent date of publication.

We made some operations to facilitate the analysis :

  • Any operation on the corpus is case insensitive (uppercase / lowercase).
  • A list of irrelevant words has been removed from the corpus (Attachment 7.1).
  • Deletion of punctuation.

Temporal study of the occurrence of terms containing "Islam" in the sub-corpus made using Python Pandas. The Le Monde sub-corpus (19,981 documents) is quantitatively more important than that of Libération (9,641 documents) or Le Figaro (11,037 documents).

After having been annotated to associate with its grammatical form (Part-of-Speech Tagging), the corpus was subjected to matrix processing (DTM) via the python library Sklearn, making it possible to count the number of occurrences for each lemma.

4.5. The adjectives associated with "Islam" in the corpus.

The matrix (see 4.3) lists terms but also group of terms (or ngrams). Here are identified the most commonly used adjectives (at 1 word of interval maximum) before or after the lemma "Islam".

Only the 300 most used adjectives were the subject of an editorial treatment.
